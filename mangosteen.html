Mangosteen Maturity Detection App

A desktop application applying image processing to aid the agricultural industry.

Project Background

Manually sorting fruit by maturity is a time-consuming and subjective process in the agricultural sector. This project aimed to automate the detection of mangosteen maturity by analyzing the fruit's skin color, providing a more accurate and efficient method for quality control.

Technology Stack

Challenges & Solutions

The main technical challenge was accurately distinguishing between ripe and unripe fruit under varying lighting conditions. Simple RGB color analysis was unreliable.

Solution: I implemented image processing using the HSV (Hue, Saturation, Value) color space, which is less sensitive to lighting changes. By applying thresholding techniques on the Hue channel, I could isolate the specific color range of ripe mangosteens. I then used morphological operations in OpenCV, such as erosion and dilation, to remove noise and produce a clean binary mask, clearly distinguishing between mature and immature fruits.
